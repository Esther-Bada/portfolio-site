About Me

Educator, builder, and lifelong learner — figuring out what tech can do for people.

My path into tech didn’t start with code — it started with children.

I studied Early Childhood Education at the University of Lagos, where I spent years trying to understand how children learn, what makes knowledge stick, and why some are left behind before they’ve even had a fair chance.

While I was still in school, I began exploring cloud computing through AWS certifications. That experience introduced me to a different side of technology, not just using tools, but understanding how they are built and how they scale.

In my final year, I carried that curiosity into my research, working on game-based digital tools and their impact on children’s literacy. It became clear to me that technology can either widen the gap in learning or help close it, depending on how it’s used.

Beyond academics, I volunteered as a lay counsellor with Blaze Health Foundation, supporting students navigating anxiety, depression, and mental health challenges. That experience shaped how I think about building, with empathy, not assumptions.

Currently, I am observing my mandatory NYSC program, where I serve in the education sector, teaching at a nursery and primary school in Lagos while continuing to build and explore AI tools and cloud systems.

I’m not trying to leave education behind — I’m carrying it with me into every system I design and every problem I choose to solve.

Because for me, the goal isn’t just to work in tech. It’s to use technology to make learning more accessible, more human, and more equitable.
